5 minutes total, every 2-4 weeks
STEP 1:

Twist open the chamber of the device and fill with one ampule of serum. Twist on needle head with lid to close it tight.

STEP 2:

Leave the device upside down for 1-2 minutes. During this time, wash your face and make sure your hands are clean.

STEP 3:

Once the serum has had enough time to make its way into the needles, you can take off the lid and start micro-infusing!

STEP 4:

Start at the centre of the face and work your way outward to cover the full face. Take any leftover serum from the device and pat it onto your face and neck to leave on overnight.

STEP 5:

Once treatment is completed, place lid back on the needle head. Twist the lid off and dispose safely. Open a new sterile needle head and put it on the device ready for your next treatment.

What you should know for the best results

The Qure Micro-Infusion System is designed with your safety in mind.

Unlike popular at-home derma rollers, our stamper device features single-use sterile heads that help minimise the risk of infection or unnecessary skin damage caused by blunt and multi-use needles.

For more information on the treatment protocol, please click button below for the full treatment guide.

Below are a few rules you should follow while using your Qure Micro-Infusion System:

DO+ SAFETY GUIDELINES

Before treatment:

  • Perform patch test first to have a better understanding of how your skin will react
  • Ensure your face and hands are clean

After treatment:

  • Apply the rest of the serum post-treatment and leave on overnight
  • Dispose the needle head safely and use new needle head per treatment
  • Wash out the serum chamber
DONT+ SAFETY GUIDELINES

Before treatment:

  • Avoid stamping directly on active breakouts
  • Don’t use with serums other than those in Qure’s system

After treatment:

  • Avoid being in the sun for 24 hours
  • Avoid touching face with dirty hands
  • Avoid acids and exfoliating products for 24 hours

Micro-needling focuses on stimulating your body's own collagen by creating micro-injuries. But it doesn’t deliver any ingredients directly into your skin.

With microinfusion, you not only reap the benefits of micro-needling, but also deliver active ingredient solutions to target your specific skin concerns - This makes it work quicker and more effectively.

After you’ve infused your skin with all that skin boosting serum, try to keep the skin clean for 24 hours and follow these post treatment guidelines:

  • The day after treatment, wash your face with a gentle cleanser and apply a nourishing moisturizer.
  • Users may experience minor redness and irritation after the procedure but should quickly subside. Skin may experience mild swelling, peeling or flaking. You can minimize these effects by keeping the skin moisturized, which will reduce shedding and alleviate tightness.
  • Skip makeup for as long as you can, but at minimum, at least 24 hours.
  • No exercising or strenuous activity for the first 24 hours post-treatment. Sweating and gym environments are harmful, rife with bacteria, and may cause adverse reactions.
  • Keep in mind, anything you put on your skin after using Qure Microinfusion will absorb more easily, so it’s important to keep the skin clean and use products that don’t have harsh ingredients (no acids!).
  • Avoid touching your face, especially with dirty hands that haven’t been sanitized.
  • Avoid swimming, saunas and steam rooms for at least 24 hours post-treatment.
  • Avoid applying skincare products containing fragrances, active ingredients or acids such as Alpha Hydroxy Acids, Beta Hydroxy Acid, Retinol (Vitamin A), Vitamin C or anything perceived as ‘active’ skincare including scrubs or toners for at least 2-3 days post-treatment, as it may cause irritation.
  • Avoid exfoliating products for 2-3 days after treatment because they can be abrasive on skin when it is in that kind of condition.
  • Avoid direct sun exposure immediately after treatment. Apply a high protection sunscreen when going outdoors, and avoid prolonged sun exposure. Use good quality non-comedogenic sunscreen if going in the sun.

By the next day, you should be free to resume your normal activities, skincare routine, and any makeup you want to apply—but you may not even need it. Just ensure you monitor your skin’s reaction.
